Zhengjia (Peter) Sun孙正嘉

Studying how machines learn to see — with as little supervision as the world is willing to give.

I am an undergraduate at the University of Michigan working on machine learning research at the intersection of self-supervised representation learning and computer vision. I am drawn to problems where the data is abundant but the labels are scarce — biomedical imaging, scientific computing, and other domains where collecting ground truth is prohibitively expensive.

Most recently, at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, I worked on a 3D convolutional pipeline for predicting transient temperature fields in high-temperature components — a project that taught me how much of machine-learning research is really data-engineering research in disguise. Alongside it I have been deepening my fluency in the systems layer underneath ML, with a CUDA convolution-optimization project for the GPU-programming course at Michigan. I am currently doing further research in self-supervised representation learning at Michigan; this work is not yet public, but I am happy to discuss it with prospective advisors directly.

I am applying to Ph.D. programs in computer science, with a focus on machine learning, computer vision, and ML systems. My academic path has taken me through the UM-SJTU Joint Institute, exchange terms at Korea University and TU Berlin, and now Ann Arbor — which has given me an unusually broad view of how the same technical problems are framed across very different intellectual traditions.
